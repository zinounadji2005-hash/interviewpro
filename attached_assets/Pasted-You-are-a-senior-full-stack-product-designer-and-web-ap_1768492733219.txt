You are a senior full-stack product designer and web application architect.
Your task is to design and generate a complete MVP web application focused on helping job seekers successfully pass job interviews.

Product Overview

Build a web-based platform that assists users in improving their job interview performance through CV optimization and AI-powered mock interviews with performance evaluation and improvement tracking.

The application must be production-oriented, scalable, and user-friendly, but limited strictly to the MVP scope described below.

Core Objectives

Improve the quality and effectiveness of user CVs.

Simulate realistic job interview experiences.

Identify user weaknesses and provide actionable feedback.

Measure improvement between interview attempts.

Deliver personalized career and interview advice.

Functional Requirements (MVP)
1. Landing Page

Clear value proposition explaining how the platform helps users pass job interviews.

Call-to-action buttons:

“Get Started”

“Sign Up”

“Log In”

Simple, professional, and trustworthy UI.

Brief explanation of the process:

Upload CV → Improve CV → Mock Interview → Feedback → Second Interview → Progress Evaluation.

2. Authentication

User registration and login system.

Email + password authentication (social login optional but not required for MVP).

Secure session handling.

3. Main Dashboard (Post-Login)

This is the primary user workspace.

Features:

Upload CV (PDF / DOCX).

CV analysis and optimization:

Improve wording

Strengthen achievements

Optimize for ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems)

Provide explanations for suggested changes.

Display original vs improved CV comparison.

4. AI-Powered Mock Interview – Round 1

Conduct an initial virtual interview based on:

User’s CV

Selected job role (if provided) or generic role otherwise

Interview format:

Text-based or voice-based (choose one for MVP).

Ask realistic interview questions (behavioral + technical if applicable).

Capture user responses.

5. Interview Evaluation & Feedback

After the first interview:

Analyze answers for:

Clarity

Confidence

Relevance

Structure (STAR method, etc.)

Identify weaknesses and mistakes.

Provide:

Clear improvement points

Example better answers

Communication tips

6. AI-Powered Mock Interview – Round 2

Conduct a second interview after feedback.

Questions should be:

Similar in difficulty

Slightly varied to avoid memorization

Compare performance against the first interview.

7. Improvement Analysis & Advice

Show comparison results:

Improvement areas

Remaining weaknesses

Provide:

Personalized interview tips

CV-related advice

General job interview guidance

Non-Functional Requirements

Clean, modern UI (professional career-focused design).

Responsive (desktop-first, mobile-friendly).

Modular architecture.

Clear separation between:

Frontend

Backend

AI logic

Easy to extend in future versions.

Constraints

This is an MVP:

No payment system.

No admin panel.

No advanced analytics.

Focus on correctness, usability, and clarity over feature quantity.

Deliverables Expected from You

Full application structure.

UI/UX layout and page flow.

Recommended tech stack.

Core backend logic.

AI interaction logic and prompts.

Minimal but functional implementation ready for testing.

Act as if this product will be used by real job seekers.
Prioritize realism, clarity, and measurable improvement.
